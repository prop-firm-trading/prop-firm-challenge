A prop firm challenge is a paid evaluation. You prove you can trade profitably within a set of rules. If you pass, the firm funds an account for you to trade with their capital.

That is the simple version. The reality is more nuanced, and most people fail.

This guide explains how challenges work, what the rules actually mean, and how to pick the right one.

---

## The Two-Phase Model

Most prop firms use a two-phase evaluation. Some use one phase. A few offer instant funding (skip the evaluation for a premium).

### Standard Two-Phase Structure

| Phase | Goal | Typical Rules |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Phase 1 (Evaluation) | Hit profit target (8-10%) | Max drawdown 8-12%, daily drawdown 4-5%, time limit varies |
| Phase 2 (Verification) | Hit smaller target (5-8%) | Same drawdown rules, no minimum trading days |
| Funded Account | Trade firm capital | Keep 80-95% of profits, bi-weekly payouts, no time limit |

---

## The Four Key Rules

Every prop firm challenge has four core rules. Understanding these is the difference between passing and failing.

### 1. Maximum Drawdown

The total amount you can lose before the challenge ends. Usually 8-12% of the starting balance.

**Static drawdown:** Fixed at the starting balance. If you start with $100k and the max is 10%, you cannot go below $90k at any point. This is easier because profits are yours to keep.

**Trailing drawdown:** Moves up with your profits. If your $100k account grows to $110k, your drawdown limit moves to $99k (10% of $110k). This locks in gains but makes recovery from losses harder.

> **Tip:** Choose firms with static drawdown for your first challenge. Trailing drawdown increases the difficulty significantly.

### 2. Daily Drawdown

How much you can lose in a single trading day. Usually 4-5%.

This is the rule that catches most traders. A single bad trade can end your entire challenge, even if your overall P&L is positive.

**Example:** You have a $100k account with 5% daily drawdown. Your account starts the day at $95k. If it drops below $90,250 (5% of $95k), you fail. The daily drawdown resets each day based on that day's starting equity.

### 3. Profit Target

How much profit you need to make. Usually 8-10% in Phase 1 and 5-8% in Phase 2.

Lower is better. An 8% target is significantly easier than 10%. Some firms offer 6% targets which are achievable with conservative trading.

### 4. Time Limit

Some firms give 30 days per phase. Others have no time limit.

No time limit is easier because it removes the pressure to trade. But it can also lead to overtrading as you try to end the phase quickly. Self-discipline still matters.

## Common Reasons People Fail

### 1. Ignoring The Daily Drawdown

The most common failure mode. A trader has a winning week (up 6%), then a single bad day (down 4.5%). The daily drawdown limit was 5%. They are still up overall, but the challenge is over.

**Fix:** Set a hard stop at 80% of the daily drawdown limit. If your limit is 5%, stop trading at 4%.

### 2. Overtrading To Beat The Clock

The 30 day time limit makes traders rush. They take low-probability setups, oversize positions, and blow through drawdown limits.

**Fix:** If the time limit stresses you, pick a firm with no time limit (FundedNext, E8, Apex).

### 3. Wrong Position Sizing

Risking 2-3% per trade on a challenge with 5% daily drawdown means two losing trades end your day. Three losing days in a row means you fail at the weekly level.

**Fix:** Risk no more than 0.5-1% per trade. This protects your daily drawdown and gives you room to recover from losses.

### 4. Chasing Losses

A losing trade triggers the urge to double down on the next one to recover fast. This is how people blow through daily drawdown limits in 15 minutes.

**Fix:** Accept losses. Close the charts. Come back tomorrow. A challenge is a marathon, not a sprint.

### 5. Not Reading The Fine Print

Hidden consistency rules, trailing drawdown, payout minimums, country restrictions. Traders buy a challenge without reading the terms and fail because of a rule they did not know existed.

**Fix:** Read the full terms before buying. Check Trustpilot and trader forums for reviews.

## Cost Per Attempt Strategy

Prop firm challenges have a low pass rate (estimated below 20%). Budget for multiple attempts.

**Recommended approach:** Start with the cheapest option (FundedNext at $39 or Apex at $65). Learn the rules. Fail a few times cheaply. When you are consistently hitting targets on demo, move to a larger account.

Budget $200-300 for your first 3-5 attempts across cheap firms. Do not start with a $540 FTMO challenge as your first try.
