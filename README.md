A prop firm challenge is a paid evaluation. You prove you can trade profitably within a set of rules. If you pass, the firm gives you a funded account to trade with their capital.

That is the simple version. The reality is more nuanced.

---

### How Challenges Work

Most prop firms use a two-phase model:

| Phase | What You Do | What Happens |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Phase 1 | Hit profit target (8-10%) within rules | Pass = Move to Phase 2 |
| Phase 2 | Hit smaller target (5-8%) | Pass = Get funded |
| Funded Account | Trade firm capital, keep 80-95% of profits | Bi-weekly payouts |

Some firms offer one-phase challenges. Some offer instant funding (skip the evaluation and pay a premium). Each model has trade-offs.

---

### The Key Rules To Understand

**1. Max Drawdown**

The total amount you can lose. Usually 8-12% of starting balance. If you hit it, you fail.

- Static drawdown: Fixed at starting balance. Easier.
- Trailing drawdown: Moves up with your profits. Harder.

**2. Daily Drawdown**

How much you can lose in a single day. Usually 4-5%. This is the rule that catches most traders. A single bad day can end your challenge.

**3. Profit Target**

How much you need to make. Usually 8-10%. Lower is better. Some firms offer 6-8% targets which are significantly easier to hit.

**4. Time Limit**

Some firms give you 30 days per phase. Others have no time limit. No time limit is easier but can lead to overtrading as you try to end the phase fast.

---

### Common Traps

- Consistency rules that force you to hit profit targets in a specific way. Avoid these firms.
- Trailing drawdown that locks in losses. Hard to recover from.
- Hidden fees on payout requests.
- Payout delays. Check Trustpilot before buying.

### Which Challenge To Pick

- **First time?** FundedNext or E8 Funding. Low cost, low targets, no time limit.
- **Experienced?** FTMO is the gold standard. Higher fees but proven payouts.
- **Futures trader?** Apex Trader Funding. 100% profit split on first $10k.
